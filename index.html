Your Adventure Awaits

Embark on an unforgettable cycling adventure from the heart of the nation's capital to the stunning shores of Sydney. The Tour de NSW is more than just a ride; it's a four-day challenge that will take you through rolling hills, charming country towns, breathtaking descents, and the lush beauty of the Royal National Park. And best of all, you will raise money for a good cause. Are you ready to ride?

The Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 0: 30 March 2027

Coach transfer from Sydney to Canberra

For riders starting their journey from Sydney, a comfortable coach transfer will be arranged to take you to our starting point in Canberra. This is a great chance to meet fellow cyclists before the adventure begins. Bikes will be transported separately via truck. Carfully consider your travel arrangements - you either need to be in Sydney on the morning of 30 March 2027 to catch a coach to Canberra, or meet us in Canberra on 30 March 2027.

Day 1: 31 March 2027

Canberra to Goulburn

114km

We roll out from the capital, leaving city life behind for the open roads of New South Wales. Today's stage is a challenging but rewarding ride through classic Australian countryside, setting the tone for the adventure ahead.

Day 2: 1 April 2027

Goulburn to Bowral

88km

A shorter day in the saddle brings us into the heart of the Southern Highlands. We'll pedal through charming villages and picturesque farmland before arriving in the beautiful town of Bowral, known for its stunning gardens and country hospitality.

Day 3: 2 April 2027

Bowral to Wollongong

70km Downhill Sprint

Get ready for an exhilarating ride! Today is all about the descent. We leave the highlands behind and enjoy a thrilling downhill run towards the coast, with spectacular views of the ocean opening up before us as we approach Wollongong.

Day 4: 3 April 2027

Wollongong to Sydney

90km Queen Stage

The final push, the Queen Stage! We tackle the iconic route through the magnificent Royal National Park. Winding roads, dense forest, and coastal vistas make this a truly world-class ride, finishing in triumph in the heart of Sydney.

We are proud to be supporting the Sydney Children's Hospitals Foundation. Every kilometre we ride and every dollar we raise will help provide all children with access to the best possible healthcare, whenever and wherever they need it.

Fundraising is a key component of this challenge. We ask each rider to do their best to raise money for the SCHF. A dedicated fundraising page will be set up for each participant, making it easy to share your journey and collect donations from friends, family, and colleagues.

  • Do not underestimate the challenge.
  • It is recommended that all cyclists should complete a ride local to them with at least 1000m of elevation gain over 100km of cycling.
  • Strava accounts will be requested for all riders - if you don't have one yet, please create one as organisers will be monitoring training progress.
  • Regular long distance rides will be organised for participants from Sydney and Perth - be sure to join these to ensure your training is on track.

On The Bike (Mandatory)

  • Roadworthy bike
  • Cycling helmet (must be worn at all times when riding)
  • Cycling gloves
  • Two water bottles (minimum)
  • Front and rear lights
  • Small saddle bag with: 2x spare inner tubes, tyre levers, multi-tool
  • Hi-viz jersey (will be provided)
  • Padded cycling shorts (bibs recommended)

Off The Bike

  • Casual clothing for evenings
  • Toiletries
  • Sunscreen
  • Any personal medications
  • Phone and chargers

    • This is not an exhaustive list - we will be adding to it as we continue planning for the event.

  • A road bike is highly recommended for this event. Hybrid bikes are acceptable but will be more challenging. Mountain bikes or e-bikes are not permitted for safety reasons.
  • Please ensure your bike is fully serviced before the event. Check your brakes, gears, and tyres for wear.
  • There will be long and steep descents on the route - disc brakes are recommended but rim brakes are also okay. Regardless, we ask that all riders take care and remain in full control of their bikes at all times.
  • Mechanical support will be available, but you should be able to fix a simple puncture yourself. Please carry the necessary spares (tubes, levers, pump/CO2).

If you are travelling from interstate or overseas, hiring a bike may be a convenient option. There are several excellent bike hire services in Sydney. We recommend booking well in advance. We will post some options here over the next few months.
